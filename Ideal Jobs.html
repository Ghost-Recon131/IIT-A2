Overview
Looking through everyone’s ideal job, one core skill that is consistently required is having good communication skills. Having good communication skills is vital as it has applications to most if not all jobs. Being able to effectively communicate with your peers and colleagues allows for a better understanding when it comes to tackling a certain topic or problem. In certain scenarios where a part of the job isn’t clear, having someone else to explain or provide another outlook on the problem can vastly improve the way one deals with said problem and improve the quality of the work. In more collaborative situations having good communication allows teams to know the pace that the work is being done at. It helps determine what tasks have been done and ones that need to be worked on to ensure the job is done on time. There are other soft skills such as teamwork, learning mentality and adaptability which also pop up but none as prominent as having good communication skills. For the other skills, since everyone had different ideal jobs, the skills required vary between each person. Each job had different skills and experience needed to suit the requirements so there were very few times when certain members had to develop similar skills to each other apart from the soft skills due to the difference of the jobs. Each plan is similar to each other as each person identifies what they are currently lacking for their ideal job, then provide a general plan for how they want to tackle and obtain the necessary skills and experience required for each job.
